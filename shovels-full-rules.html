Shovels

A build‑and‑battle card game for 2–4 players (add decks for more).

1. Components

Ranks: 2 – 10 as printed, Ace = 10.
Suits: ♣ Clubs • ♦ Diamonds • ♥ Hearts • ♠ Spades.

2. Goal

Be the last player with at least one living character on the table.

3. Setup

  1. Build the main deck.
    1. Combine both 52‑card decks (104 cards).
    2. Pull out all Jacks, Queens, Kings (24 cards).
      • Shuffle them; deal 3 face‑down to each player (these are your characters).
      • Return the rest to the main deck, then shuffle thoroughly.
  2. Create the Shop pile. Deal 20 face‑down cards; set aside for Phase 2.
  3. Central deck & discard. Put the remaining deck within reach, leaving space beside it for a face‑up discard stack.
  4. Choose the start player (last winner, or last person who used a real shovel). Give them the token.

4. Phase 1 — Character Creation

Each face card is a character. Build three vertical stacks in your hand or behind the divider:

Stacks are fully visible in later phases; fan them downward or sideways so every card is readable.

Turn sequence — clockwise

  1. Draw exactly two new cards:
    • EITHER both from the face‑down deck
    • OR one or two from the top of the discard pile, then from the deck until you hold two.
  2. Discard one of those two to the face‑up pile.
  3. Play the other:
    • Number card → place it on top of any character stack you own.
    • Face card → replace a face on one of your stacks (old face is discarded). The rest of that stack is untouched. You may instead discard the new face if you wish.

Repeat until the deck is empty. Continue turn order (discard‑pile draws only) until everyone has taken the same number of turns. Reveal any hidden stacks; proceed to battle!

5. Phase 2 — Battle

5.1 Prepare the Shop

  1. Shuffle the discard pile with the 20‑card Shop pile; place face‑down.
  2. Flip three cards face‑up beside it — this is the Shop row.

5.2 Determine the first active player

Whoever shows the highest‑rank ♠ in any stack starts (Ace > 10 > 9 … 2). Ignore face cards. Break ties by the next‑lower Spade; if still tied, keep the original token holder.

5.3 Your Turn (overview)

  1. Choose a living, untapped character.
  2. EITHER form an action hand (see 5.4), OR tap its hero power (see 7).
  3. End of turn: refill empty Shop slots so three face‑up cards are ready for the next player.

5.4 Action Hand (discarding)

  1. Lift one or more cards from the top of that stack; these are “in hand.”
  2. Pick a suit present in your held cards.
  3. Add their ranks (Ace = 10) and resolve the matching effect:

This counts as “removing a card from the table” for the Must‑Act rule.

6. Shopping

You may enter the shop during a Diamonds action or via certain hero powers.

6.1 Prices

  • Number card = its rank (Ace 10).
  • Face cards — Jack 3 • Queen 4 • King 5.

6.2 Buying & placing

  • Pay coins, take card, place immediately on top of any living character you own.
  • Upgrading royalty must rise in rank (J → Q/K, Q → K). To create a brand‑new stack (max 3) you must start with a Jack.

6.3 Refreshing the row

  • During your shop visit you may pay 2 coins once or multiple times to wipe the row, discarding any remaining cards and flipping three replacements.
  • End‑of‑turn auto‑refill: After you finish shopping, immediately fill empty slots so the next player always starts with three face‑up options.

7. Hero Powers — “Tap Once per Life”

Tapping a face counts as your whole turn (no discard). A face may tap once per current life; replacing that face card resets it.

8. Must‑Act & Fatigue

Every turn you must either:

  • Discard at least one card from a stack (forming an action hand, even if that hand doesn't produce any meaningful effects), or
  • Tap a hero power, or
  • Use a free 1‑damage face‑strike that successfully kills a card.

If no legal option exists, discard one of your own face cards — that character dies.

9. End of the Game

When a single player is the only one with living characters, they win. Shuffle everything and go again!

10. Variants

  • Mass Armies. New face cards create additional characters instead of replacing. Great chaos for 5+ player games.
  • Bigger Tables. Add one full 52‑card deck for every extra pair of players beyond four.
  • Speed Round. Use a single deck in a 1v1 and lower the act ive character count per player to 2 for a much faster game. Reduce the number of cards set aside during the build phase to 10.
